4TH SEMISTER PHYSICS

Unit 1: Waves

  • Fundamentals of Waves: Covers the basic properties and characteristics of waves, such as wavelength, frequency, amplitude, and wave velocity.
  • Transverse Wave Propagation Along a Stretched String: Discusses the behavior of transverse waves traveling along a string or medium.
  • General Solution of the Wave Equation and Its Significance: Explains the general solution of the wave equation and its importance in understanding wave propagation.
  • Modes of Vibration of a Stretched String Clamped at Ends: Describes the different vibrational patterns or modes that a stretched string exhibits when fixed at both ends.
  • Overtones: Focuses on the higher harmonics or overtones present in the vibration of a string or musical instrument.
  • Energy Transport: Explores how energy is transferred through waves and the concept of wave energy.
  • Transverse Impedance: Discusses the opposition or resistance encountered by a transverse wave as it propagates through a medium.

Unit 2: Interference

  • Principle of Superposition: Explains the principle of superposition, which states that when two or more waves meet, their displacements combine algebraically.
  • Coherence: Discusses the coherence of waves and the conditions required for interference to occur.
  • Temporal Coherence and Spatial Coherence: Differentiates between temporal coherence (related to time) and spatial coherence (related to space) in wave interference phenomena.
  • Conditions for Interference of Light: Explains the necessary conditions for interference to occur in the context of light waves.
  • Interference by Division of Wavefront: Describes the interference pattern created when a wavefront is divided and recombined.
  • Fresnel's Biprism: Discusses Fresnel's biprism setup, which is used to create interference fringes.
  • Determination of Wavelength of Light: Explores how interference patterns can be used to determine the wavelength of light.
  • Determination of Thickness of a Transparent Material Using Biprism: Discusses how the interference pattern produced by a transparent material can be used to determine its thickness.

Unit 3: Diffraction

  • Introduction: Provides an overview of diffraction, which is the bending and spreading of waves around obstacles or through openings
  • Distinction between Fresnel and Fraunhofer Diffraction: Differentiates between Fresnel diffraction (near-field) and Fraunhofer diffraction (far-field) phenomena.
  • Fraunhofer Diffraction: Explains diffraction patterns produced when waves pass through small apertures or slits.
  • Diffraction Due to Single Slit and Circular Aperture: Discusses the diffraction patterns produced by a single slit and a circular aperture.
  • Limit of Resolution: Explores the limit of resolution determined by the diffraction of waves.
  • Fraunhofer Diffraction Due to Double Slit: Describes the interference and diffraction patterns created by double slits.
  • Fraunhofer Diffraction Pattern with N Slits: Discusses the diffraction pattern produced by multiple slits.
  • Resolving Power of Grating: Explores the resolving power or ability of a grating to distinguish closely spaced wavelengths of light.
  • Determination of Wavelength of Light Using Diffraction Grating: Explains how diffraction gratings can be used to determine the wavelength of light.

Unit 4: Polarization

  • Polarized Light: Explains polarized light, which refers to light waves that vibrate in a specific direction.
  • Methods of Polarization: Discusses various methods to polarize light, such as using polarizers or selective absorption.
  • Polarization by Reflection: Describes the phenomenon of polarization that occurs when light is reflected off a surface.
  • Diffraction: Covers the polarization of light due to diffraction, which occurs when light passes through small openings or obstacles.
  • Double Refraction: Explains the phenomenon of double refraction, where a beam of light splits into two polarized beams when passing through certain materials.
  • Selective Absorption: Discusses the process of selective absorption, where certain materials absorb specific polarizations of light.
  • Scattering of Light: Explores the scattering of light by small particles, which can result in the polarization of scattered light.
  • Brewster's Law: Describes Brewster's law, which relates the angle of incidence and the polarization state of reflected light.
  • Malus' Law: Discusses Malus' law, which describes the intensity of light transmitted through a polarizer as a function of the angle between the polarizer and the incident light.
  • Nicol Prism: Explains the Nicol prism, a type of polarizing prism used to separate polarized light into two components.
  • Polarizer and Analyzer: Discusses the use of polarizers and analyzers in polarization experiments to control and analyze the polarization state of light.
  • Refraction of Plane Wave Incident on Negative and Positive Crystals: Explores the behavior of polarized light when it undergoes refraction in negative and positive crystals.
  • Quarter Wave Plate: Describes the properties and functions of a quarter-wave plate, which introduces a quarter-wavelength phase difference between two orthogonal polarization components.
  • Half-Wave Plate: Explains the properties and functions of a half-wave plate, which introduces a half-wavelength phase difference between two orthogonal polarization components.
