6TH SEMISTER CYBER LAW

Here is a brief explanation of the topics covered in each unit of the subject "Information Security & Cyber Laws":


UNIT-I: Introduction to Information System and Security

  • Computer Networks: Refers to the interconnected system of computers and other devices that enables communication and data exchange.
  • Internet: A global network of interconnected computer networks that allows communication and access to information worldwide.
  • Protocol: A set of rules and procedures that govern the communication between devices in a network.
  • Network Core: The central part of a computer network that handles data routing and switching.
  • Information System: A system that collects, processes, stores, and disseminates information to support business operations.
  • Types of IS: Different categories of information systems, such as transaction processing systems, management information systems, decision support systems, etc.
  • Information Security: Measures and practices implemented to protect information from unauthorized access, use, disclosure, disruption, modification, or destruction.
  • Need for Information Security: The reasons why organizations need to prioritize information security, including protecting sensitive data, maintaining business continuity, complying with regulations, and preserving reputation.
  • Information Assurance: The processes and practices aimed at ensuring the confidentiality, integrity, availability, and authenticity of information and systems.
  • Cybersecurity: The practice of protecting computers, servers, mobile devices, electronic systems, networks, and data from digital attacks and unauthorized access.
  • Tools of the Attacker: Techniques and tools used by malicious individuals to compromise the security of information systems, such as malware, social engineering, phishing, etc.
  • Scanning and Spoofing: Techniques used to scan for vulnerabilities in networks and systems and to deceive or impersonate entities in order to gain unauthorized access.
  • Password Cracking: The process of discovering or guessing a password to gain unauthorized access to a system or account.
  • Malicious Software: Software designed to harm or exploit computer systems or users, including viruses, worms, Trojans, ransomware, etc.
  • Session Hijacking: Unauthorized takeover of a legitimate user's session in a computer network or application.

UNIT-II: Introduction to Cryptography and Applications

  • Introduction to Application Security: An overview of the security considerations and practices for developing secure software applications.
  • Data Security Considerations: Factors to consider when protecting sensitive data, including data encryption, access controls, secure storage, etc.
  • Security Technologies: Tools, techniques, and mechanisms used to protect information and systems, such as firewalls, intrusion detection systems, antivirus software, etc.
  • Important Terms: Definitions of key terms related to cryptography and application security, such as threats, flaws, vulnerabilities, attacks, ciphers, etc.
  • Private Key Cryptography: A cryptographic system that uses a single secret key to both encrypt and decrypt data.
  • Substitution Cipher (Caesar): A simple encryption technique that replaces each letter in the plaintext with a letter a fixed number of positions down the alphabet.
  • Transposition (Rail-Fence): A cipher that rearranges the order of characters in the plaintext to create the ciphertext.
  • Security Threats to E-Commerce: The specific risks and challenges faced by electronic commerce platforms, including data breaches, identity theft, fraud, etc.
  • E-Cash and Electronic Payment System: An overview of electronic payment methods and digital currency systems.
  • Credit/Debit/Smart Cards: Different types of cards used for electronic transactions, their features, and security considerations.
  • Forensics: The process of collecting, analyzing, and preserving digital evidence for legal purposes.
  • Digital Signature: A cryptographic mechanism used to verify the authenticity and integrity of digital documents and messages.

UNIT-III: Introduction to Security Measures

  • Secure Information System Development: Best practices and methodologies for developing secure information systems, including secure coding, testing, and deployment processes.
  • Application Development Security: Security considerations and practices specific to software development, such as input validation, secure configuration, secure coding practices, etc.
  • Security Information: The process of identifying, collecting, analyzing, and disseminating information about security threats, vulnerabilities, and incidents.
  • Security Governance: The framework, policies, procedures, and practices for managing and overseeing an organization's information security efforts.
  • Risk Management: The process of identifying, assessing, and mitigating risks to information systems and assets.
  • Security Architecture and Design: The design principles, concepts, and components that ensure the security of an information system.
  • Security Issues in Hardware: Security vulnerabilities and considerations related to computer hardware components, such as processors, memory, storage devices, etc.
  • Data Storage: Secure storage practices for protecting sensitive data from unauthorized access or loss.
  • Downloadable Devices: Security challenges associated with external devices, such as USB drives, external hard drives, etc.
  • Physical Security of IT Assets: Measures and controls to protect physical assets, such as servers, network equipment, and data centers.
  • Backup Security Measures: Strategies and practices for securely backing up and restoring data to prevent data loss or unauthorized access.

UNIT-IV: Introduction to Security Policies and Cyber Laws

  • Need for an Information Security Policy: The reasons why organizations should have a comprehensive and well-defined information security policy.
  • Security Standards ISO: Overview of the International Organization for Standardization (ISO) standards related to information security.
  • Introducing Various Security Policies and Their Review Process: Different types of security policies, their components, and the process of reviewing and updating them.
  • Introduction to Indian Cyberlaw: An overview of the Information Technology Act (IT Act) in India, its objectives, and scope.
  • Intellectual Property Issues: Legal aspects related to intellectual property rights, including patents, copyrights, and software licensing.
  • Overview of Intellectual Property-Related Legislation in India: A summary of the key legislation in India concerning intellectual property rights.
  • Patent: Legal protection granted to inventors for their inventions, giving them exclusive rights for a specified period.
  • Copyright: Legal protection granted to the creators of original works, such as literary, artistic, musical, or software works.
  • Software License: Legal agreement between the software copyright holder and the end-user, defining the terms of software usage and distribution.
