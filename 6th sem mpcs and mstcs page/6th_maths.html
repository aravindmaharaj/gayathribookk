6TH SEMISTER MATHEMATICS

UNIT-1: Sphere

  • Definition of a Sphere: A sphere is a three-dimensional solid figure that is perfectly round and bounded by a closed surface. Every point on the surface of a sphere is equidistant from a fixed point called the center.
  • Various Forms of the Sphere: Different representations of a sphere, such as in terms of its radius, diameter, or center coordinates.
  • The Given Sphere through Four Points: Exploring the conditions under which a sphere can be uniquely determined by four given points.
  • Intercept Form of the Sphere: Deriving the equation of a sphere in intercept form, which represents the sphere in terms of its intersection points with the coordinate axes.
  • Equation of Circle of Intersection of a Plane and Sphere: Finding the equation of the circle formed by the intersection of a plane and a sphere.
  • Line Equation of a Tangent Plane: Determining the equation of a tangent plane to a sphere at a given point.
  • Angle of Intersection of Two Spheres: Analyzing the conditions for two spheres to intersect and finding the angle of intersection.
  • Limiting Points: Understanding the concept of limiting points and their relationship to spheres.
  • Cone Cyclic Points: Exploring the cyclic property of points on a cone and its significance.
  • Radical Plane: Defining the radical plane of two spheres and studying its properties.

    • UNIT-2: Cones & Cylinders

    • Definition of a Cone: Understanding the basic characteristics and properties of a cone, a three-dimensional solid with a circular base and a pointed vertex.
    • Intercept Form of Cone: Expressing the equation of a cone in terms of its intercepts on the coordinate axes.
    • Condition that the General Equation of Second Degree Represents a Cone: Investigating the conditions under which a general equation of a second degree represents a cone.
    • Cone and Plane through Vertex: Exploring the relationship between a cone and a plane passing through its vertex.
    • Intersection of a Line with a Cone: Determining the points of intersection between a line and a cone.

    UNIT-3: The Right Circular Cone and Cylinder

    • The Right Circular Cone: Studying the properties and characteristics of a right circular cone, including its slant height, lateral surface area, and volume.
    • The Cylinder: Understanding the features and properties of a cylinder, including its base, height, lateral surface area, and volume.
    • The Right Circular Cylinder: Exploring the properties of a right circular cylinder, including its base, height, lateral surface area, and volume.

    UNIT-4: Conicoid

    • The Conicoid: Defining a conicoid as a surface generated by rotating a conic section about its axis.
    • The General Equation of Second Degree: Expressing the general equation of a conicoid as a second-degree equation in three variables.
    • Various Forms of Conicoid: Exploring different forms of conicoids, such as elliptic, parabolic, and hyperbolic conicoids.
    • Intersection of a Line with Conicoid: Analyzing the points of intersection between a line and a conicoid.
    • Plane of Contact: Understanding the plane of contact between a line and a conicoid.
    • Enveloping Cone: Exploring the concept of an enveloping cone and its relationship to a conicoid.
    • Enveloping Cylinder: Studying the properties and characteristics of an enveloping cylinder.
