Unit 2: Principles of Design of Experiments

  • Replication: Repeating treatments or experimental units to reduce variability and increase reliability.
  • Randomization: Randomly assigning experimental units to treatments to minimize bias and confounding.
  • Local control: Using blocking to minimize the influence of specific factors by grouping similar units together.
  • Types of designs: Completely Randomized Design (CRD), Randomized Block Design (RBD), Factorial Design, and Latin Square Design.
  • Statistical analysis of CRD: Calculating treatment sums of squares, degrees of freedom, and conducting hypothesis testing.
  • Missing plot techniques in CRD: Methods to handle missing or unusable plots in CRD experiments.
  • RBD: Randomized Block Design incorporating blocking to reduce variability.
  • Statistical analysis of RBD: Analyzing treatment effects within blocks using appropriate statistical tests.
  • Missing plot techniques in RBD: Approaches to handle missing or unusable plots in RBD experiments.
  • Efficiency of RBD over CRD: Comparing the efficiency of RBD in reducing variability and increasing precision.
  • LSD: Least Significant Difference test for comparing treatment means in ANOVA.
  • Statistical analysis of LSD: Evaluating treatment mean differences using the LSD value.
  • Missing plot techniques of LSD: Approaches to address missing or unusable plots in LSD experiments.

Unit 3: Vital Statistics

  • Registration method: Collecting vital statistics by registering events like births, deaths, marriages, etc.
  • Census method: Obtaining vital statistics by conducting a comprehensive population count.
  • Simple survey method: Using surveys to gather vital statistics from representative samples.
  • Ratios and rates: Comparing and measuring vital events using ratios and rates.
  • Mortality rates: Quantifying death occurrences in a population.
  • Measurements of population growth: Assessing population changes using birth, death, and migration rates.
  • Life table: A statistical tool analyzing mortality and survival patterns.
  • Descriptions of life table: Components and structure of a life table.
  • Uses of life table: Applications in life expectancy estimation, mortality trend analysis, and policy planning.
  • Abridged life table: A condensed version of a life table focusing on specific age groups or populations.

Unit 4: Indian Official Statistics

  • Central Statistical Organization: The apex statistical agency in India coordinating statistical activities and providing official statistics.
  • National Sample Survey Organization: Conducting large-scale sample surveys on socio-economic indicators.
  • Yield statistics: Measurements of agricultural crop productivity.
  • Basic concepts of national income: GDP, GNP, NDP, and national income at factor cost.
  • Index numbers: Statistical tools to measure changes in variables over time or across categories.
  • Construction of index numbers: Methods for creating index numbers.
  • Criteria of good index numbers: Standards for evaluating the quality of index numbers.
  • Chain base index numbers: Index numbers calculated using a linked series of base periods.
  • Fixed base index numbers: Index numbers calculated using a fixed base period.
  • Characteristics in percentages: Expressing statistical information in percentage form.
