6TH SEMISTER PHYSICS

UNIT-1: Band Theory of P-N Junction

  • Energy Band in Solids: Exploring the concept of energy bands in solid materials and their relation to the electronic structure.
  • Valency Band: Understanding the valence band as the highest energy band containing electrons at absolute zero temperature.
  • Conduction Band: Defining the conduction band as the energy band above the valence band that allows electrons to move freely.
  • Forbidden Energy Gap in Solids: Describing the energy gap between the valence band and the conduction band, determining the conductivity properties of materials.
  • Insulators: Discussing materials with large energy gaps that restrict the movement of electrons, resulting in low electrical conductivity.
  • Semiconductors: Exploring materials with intermediate energy gaps that allow moderate electrical conductivity.
  • Intrinsic and Extrinsic Semiconductors: Differentiating between intrinsic semiconductors, which are pure materials, and extrinsic semiconductors, which are doped with impurities.
  • P-type Semiconductors: Defining p-type semiconductors, which are doped with acceptor impurities, leading to an excess of holes.
  • N-type Semiconductors: Describing n-type semiconductors, which are doped with donor impurities, resulting in an excess of electrons.
  • Fermi Level: Introducing the Fermi level as the energy level that separates filled and empty electronic states in a material.
  • Continuity Equation: Understanding the continuity equation as a mathematical expression that relates the flow of charge carriers in a semiconductor.

UNIT-2: Transistors

  • Bipolar Junction Transistor: Explaining the structure and operation of bipolar junction transistors (BJTs).
  • P-N-P, N-P-N Transistors: Differentiating between P-N-P and N-P-N transistors based on the doping of the semiconductor layers.
  • Current Components in Transistors: Analyzing the different current components (base current, collector current, emitter current) in a transistor.
  • Common Base Configuration: Describing the common base configuration of a transistor and its characteristics.
  • Common Emitter Configuration: Exploring the common emitter configuration of a transistor and its properties.
  • Common Collector Configuration: Discussing the common collector configuration of a transistor and its behavior.
  • Transistor as an Amplifier: Understanding the use of transistors as amplifiers to increase the strength of electrical signals.
  • RC Coupled Amplifier: Introducing the RC coupled amplifier circuit and its characteristics.
  • Frequency Response: Analyzing the frequency response of amplifiers and understanding the bandwidth limitations.

    • Feedback Concept & Oscillations

  • Feedback: Explaining the concept of feedback in electronic circuits and its significance in controlling gain and stability.
  • General Theory of Feedback: Discussing the general theory of feedback and its application in electronic systems.
  • Feedback Concept of Oscillators: Understanding the feedback concept in oscillators, which generate periodic waveforms.
  • Barkhausen's Criteria: Explaining Barkhausen's criteria for sustained oscillations in feedback circuits.
  • Phase Shift Oscillator: Describing the phase shift oscillator circuit and its working principle.
  • Expression for Frequency of Oscillation: Deriving the expression for the frequency of oscillation in feedback circuits.

UNIT-3: Special Devices

  • Construction and Characteristics of Photodiode: Exploring the construction and operating characteristics of photodiodes, which convert light into electrical current.
  • Shockley Diode: Introducing the Shockley diode, a four-layer diode with a controlled breakdown voltage.
  • Solar Cell: Describing the operation and characteristics of solar cells, which convert sunlight into electrical energy.
  • Optocouplers: Explaining optocouplers, also known as optoisolators, which provide electrical isolation between input and output circuits using light.
  • Field-Effect Transistor (FET): Discussing field-effect transistors and their operation based on the modulation of charge carriers in a channel.
  • FET as an Amplifier: Exploring the use of FETs as amplifiers and their advantages.
  • Unijunction Transistor (UJT): Introducing the unijunction transistor and its application as a relaxation oscillator.
  • Silicon Controlled Rectifier (SCR): Describing the structure and characteristics of SCR, a four-layer semiconductor device used as a controlled switch.

UNIT-4: Digital Electronics

  • Binary Number System: Introducing the binary number system, which represents numbers using only two digits: 0 and 1.
  • Conversion of Binary to Decimal: Explaining the process of converting binary numbers to decimal numbers.
  • Conversion of Decimal to Binary: Describing the process of converting decimal numbers to binary numbers.
  • Binary Addition and Subtraction: Exploring the methods for performing addition and subtraction operations in binary.
  • Complements: Discussing the concepts of 1's complement and 2's complement representation of binary numbers.
  • Octal Number System: Introducing the octal number system, which uses base-8 and represents numbers using digits from 0 to 7.
  • Hexadecimal Number System: Explaining the hexadecimal number system, which uses base-16 and represents numbers using digits from 0 to 9 and letters A to F.
  • Conversion of Binary to Hexadecimal: Describing the process of converting binary numbers to hexadecimal numbers.
  • Conversion of Hexadecimal to Binary: Explaining the process of converting hexadecimal numbers to binary numbers.
